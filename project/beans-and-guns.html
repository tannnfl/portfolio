Beans and Guns

Lead Designer · Online 3D Collaborative Puzzle Sandbox · 2026–Present
Game Design Tool Dev Multiplayer Play on itch.io ↗ Design Doc ↗

Overview

  • Asynchronous relay experience: each player inherits the previous player's 3D scene and places up to 5 items
  • Scene cap of 50 items; when exceeded, the oldest items are removed first (FIFO eviction)
  • Items carry hidden attributes; their sum determines the scene's proximity to one of six childhood-themed endings
  • Three phases: free creation (0–50% similarity) → locked-in ending (50–90%) → forgetting (players click items to dissolve them)
  • Visual style — color, dither size, outline weight, blur — shifts in real time via post-processing as scene attributes change
  • Dynamic music (FMOD): grows richer as ending-relevant items accumulate; fades to silence during the forgetting phase
  • Each scene contains a spirit that narrates the experience; collective goal is to free each spirit across six scenes

My Role

  • Designed the full asynchronous relay mechanic: item placement limit, FIFO eviction, fog reveal through correct combinations
  • Led gameplay expansion discussions as the project grew post-jam
  • Built Unity Editor windows for hint text authoring and item–scene relationship mapping
  • Sole implementer of hint system logic in C#
  • Participated in scene upload/download pipeline and imported two scenes with corresponding items

Design Notes

  • FIFO eviction is a generative constraint: placing something meaningful means accepting something earlier may vanish
  • Asynchronous format makes each session a conversation across time — no synchronous coordination needed
  • Six endings anchor emergent play: scenes drift toward a childhood emotional archetype even without explicit player intent
  • The forgetting phase completes the arc — creation and loss are both part of the same collective story
