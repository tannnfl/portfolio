Document Overview

Full production Game Design Document for POPLife — a 2D pixel management simulator. Covers the complete design specification for the game's core systems, authored across the full development cycle for a cross-functional team of 7+ (designers, engineers, artists).

Contents

  • Shelf & Product System: 80+ shelf types with category tiers (1–5), sell price, stock cost, maintenance fee, appeal formula, and unlock conditions
  • Bundle System: 2–4 adjacent shelves auto-activate; bundle recipes unlocked via lottery with discounted sell and stock prices
  • Customer AI: 40+ NPC profiles with 4-shelf interest sets, money bag tiers (10–300), embarrassment tracking, experience-based level upgrades, and full purchase path decision logic
  • Day Loop: Morning stock phase → real-time store hours (12:00–23:00) → end-of-day settlement panel; pause / 1× / 2× speed control
  • Store Policies: Five toggleable daily effects with explicit multipliers — Extended Hours, Category Sale, Super Sale, Advertisement, Eco-friendly Business
  • Dual-Currency Economy: Money (operations, bankruptcy trigger) + Fame (earned via quests and settlement, spent on upgrades and blueprints)
  • Quest & Narrative System: 5 story quest lines, VIP Story Missions (one active at a time), 3 concurrent daily achievements; rewards include blueprints, currency, fame, and new customers
  • Player Progression: Store star rating (0–5 stars) with shelf count, customer, and income thresholds
  • Building & Facilities: Floor and room expansion, cashiers, ATMs, air conditioners, with construction timing specs (place 15s / move 5s)
  • Storytelling & UI: NPC dialogue trees (Node Canvas), flavor text, VIP implicit puzzle narrative; UI spec includes pop bubbles, broadcast messages, and color-coded message system

Design Process

The GDD emerged from narrative-first design: NPC dialogue and story context drove what quests existed, which in turn shaped what shelf and item categories needed to be designed. This bottom-up approach kept the economy grounded in character motivation rather than abstract mechanics.
